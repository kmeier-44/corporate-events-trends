Corporate event spending has more than doubled in three years. This isn't just inflation — it reflects a fundamental shift in how companies invest in face-to-face experiences. Larger groups, higher-impact formats, and longer planning cycles are now the norm. For organisers, the message is clear: plan earlier, benchmark smarter, and compare more venues to get the best value from rising budgets.

SEASONALITY

When Events Happen

Monthly Distribution

Day of Week

The corporate events calendar follows predictable rhythms driven by budget cycles and seasonal traditions. November and December account for over a quarter of all events, fuelled by Christmas parties and year-end celebrations. June and September/October form secondary peaks as companies book summer socials and post-holiday conferences. Thursday dominates as the preferred event day — close enough to the weekend to feel celebratory, but still a working day. If you need flexibility on pricing and availability, January, April, and August are consistently the quietest months.

Understanding these seasonal fingerprints is key to smart planning. Christmas parties are almost entirely concentrated in December, which creates intense competition for venues. CATEGORY TRENDS

How the Event Mix Is Shifting

The shape of corporate events is changing. Conferences have seen the most dramatic growth — enquiry volumes quadrupled between 2022 and 2024 as companies doubled down on in-person knowledge sharing and networking. Corporate parties remain the steadiest category, reflecting consistent demand for team celebrations. Meetings, once the dominant format, have declined as companies shift budgets towards higher-impact, more experiential gatherings. Summer parties and award ceremonies are both on upward trajectories, signalling a broader move towards events that inspire and reward.

GROUP SIZES

How Big Are Corporate Events?

Average Group Size Over Time

By Event Category

Average group sizes have grown from 50 to 70 people since 2022, and the top quartile now reaches 150. This steady increase has real implications for planning: larger groups need bigger venues with more lead time, and they drive up per-event spend even when per-head costs stay flat. Award ceremonies and gala dinners skew largest at 160–200 guests, while meetings and private dining stay intimate at 20–30. If your event is growing, factor in the venue capacity constraints — the most popular large-format venues book out months ahead.

Christmas parties are the most time-sensitive events in the corporate calendar. Nearly half of all enquiries arrive in September and October — but by then, the most popular December dates at sought-after venues are already booked. Our data shows that 9% of Christmas party booking lines are lost purely because the desired date wasn't available. The takeaway is simple: start your search in the spring, confirm by July, and you'll have the widest choice of venues and dates. With median group sizes now at 90 people, early planning isn't optional — it's the difference between your first-choice venue and whatever's left.
