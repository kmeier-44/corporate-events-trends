SPENDING BENCHMARKS

What Companies Are Actually Spending

Average confirmed spend has jumped from £2,818 in 2022 to £7,230 in 2025, with the top quartile now exceeding £21,000. B2B companies and professional services firms lead the way, investing significantly more per booking than other sectors.
Understanding where your industry sits on this spectrum helps set realistic budgets, benchmark against peers, and negotiate effectively with venues.

Price per head has risen in step, climbing from £63 to £102 over the same period. Budgets are growing, but so is the cost of delivering a great experience.

SEASONALITY

When Events Happen

The corporate events calendar follows predictable rhythms driven by budget cycles and seasonal traditions. November and December account for over a quarter of all events, fuelled by Christmas parties and year-end celebrations. June and September/October form secondary peaks as companies book summer socials and post-holiday conferences.
Thursday dominates as the preferred event day — close enough to the weekend to feel celebratory, but still a working day. If you need flexibility on pricing and availability, January, April, and August are consistently the quietest months.

CATEGORY TRENDS

How the Event Mix Is Shifting

The shape of corporate events is changing. Conferences have seen the most dramatic growth — enquiry volumes quadrupled between 2022 and 2024 as companies doubled down on in-person knowledge sharing and networking.
Corporate parties remain the steadiest category, reflecting consistent demand for team celebrations. Meetings, once the dominant format, have declined as companies shift budgets towards higher-impact, more experiential gatherings.

VENUE SOURCING

Increasing Venue Option Exploration

Organisers now compare 73% more venues than they did in 2022 — up from 3.7 to 6.4 per enquiry. This isn't indecision; it's smart sourcing. With higher budgets at stake, companies are investing more time in finding the right fit.
The shift towards hotels, bars, and rooftops reflects a growing preference for venues that offer atmosphere and flexibility, not just function. If you're sourcing venues, cast a wide net early — the best options go to organisers who compare and act quickly.

GROUP SIZES

How Big Are Corporate Events?

Average group sizes have grown from 50 to 70 people since 2022, and the top quartile now reaches 150. This steady increase has real implications for planning: larger groups need bigger venues with more lead time, and they drive up per-event spend even when per-head costs stay flat.
Award ceremonies and gala dinners skew largest at 160–200 guests, while meetings and private dining stay intimate at 20–30. If your event is growing, factor in the venue capacity constraints — the most popular large-format venues book out months ahead.

CHRISTMAS PARTIES

The Christmas Party Playbook

Christmas parties are the most time-sensitive events in the corporate calendar. Nearly half of all enquiries arrive in September and October — but by then, the most popular December dates at sought-after venues are already booked. Our data shows that a significant proportion of booking lines are lost purely because the desired date wasn't available.
There's a clear pattern in group sizes too: the earliest enquiries tend to be for significantly larger groups. These organisers know that securing an exceptional venue for 100+ guests in December requires early commitment. As the year progresses, average group sizes decline — later enquirers are typically planning for smaller gatherings. Start your search in the spring, confirm by summer, and you'll have the widest choice of venues and dates.

THE BIG PICTURE

What It All Means

The corporate events market has undergone a structural transformation since 2022. Spending growth of over 150% isn't simply inflationary — it reflects a decisive shift towards larger groups, higher-impact formats, and more ambitious experiences. Companies are investing more per event because they're asking more of each gathering: stronger networking outcomes, more memorable settings, and better alignment with brand values.

Organisers who plan 3+ months ahead consistently secure better venues, stronger pricing, and more date flexibility.

Lead time expansion tells its own story. With average planning horizons now at 84 days — and conferences requiring nearly four months — early planners enjoy a compounding advantage. They access a wider pool of venues, negotiate from a position of strength, and avoid the scramble that drives up costs in peak season.

Venue sourcing behaviour has matured significantly. Organisers comparing 6+ venues per enquiry signals a more sophisticated market where due diligence is the norm, not the exception. The shift towards experiential venue types — rooftops, bars, restaurants — over traditional meeting rooms reflects broader workplace culture changes. Events are increasingly about creating moments that matter, not just filling a calendar slot.

Seasonal concentration remains one of the biggest challenges. Over a quarter of all events cluster into November and December, creating intense competition for venues and driving up prices. Understanding your event type's seasonal fingerprint — and planning counter-cyclically where possible — remains one of the simplest ways to get better value and more choice. This is especially pronounced for Christmas parties, where the largest groups start planning in Q1 while late enquirers in October face limited availability.
