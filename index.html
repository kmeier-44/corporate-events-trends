Spending

Companies are spending significantly more

The typical corporate event booking has grown from £2,800 to over £7,200 in just four years — and cost per head has risen from £63 to £102.

Actual Booking Values

Cost Per Head

For event organisers: Budget expectations are rising across the board. Private events and award ceremonies command the highest spend (£17k–18k), while meetings remain the most accessible category at around £830. Plan your budgets against these benchmarks to set realistic expectations.
Spending by Category

What different events actually cost

Typical booking value varies dramatically by event type — from £828 for meetings to over £17,000 for award ceremonies and private events.

For venues: If you're winning corporate parties at £5k but your peers in the award ceremony space are achieving £17k, consider whether your event offering could stretch into higher-value categories.
Planning

The earlier you book, the better your options

Lead times have stretched significantly since 2022, with the typical booking now placed nearly 3 months ahead. But it varies wildly by event type.

Lead Time Trend

Lead Time by Event Type

Key insight: Award ceremonies need 5+ months of planning. Conferences average nearly 4 months. Even Christmas parties — which have a fixed date — see organisers booking 3+ months out. If you're an organiser, starting your venue search late means the best spaces are already taken.
Event Types

The rise of the corporate conference

Conferences surged from just 5.5% of won bookings in 2022 to 23.8% in 2024, before settling at 10.3% in 2025. Meanwhile, meetings are declining and experience-led events like corporate parties and networking are gaining ground.

The story: Post-pandemic, companies invested heavily in bringing people together for conferences. As that wave normalised, we're seeing a diversification into networking, parties, and experiential formats — events that build culture, not just share information.
Timing

Peak windows: when to book, when to prepare

December is the busiest month, driven by Christmas parties. But the real insight is in the seasonal patterns by event type.

Monthly Distribution

Day of Week

For venues: Thursday is the single busiest event day (29%). June and September are peak conference months. Staff your sales team accordingly — and consider that August is your quietest month at just 3%.
Venue Selection

Organisers are shopping harder than ever

The average enquiry now considers 6.4 venues — up 73% from 3.7 in 2022. Over half of all enquiries are multi-venue, meaning competition for every booking is intensifying.

What this tells us: Organisers have more choice and they're using it. Your venue isn't just competing on price — it's competing on response time, proposal quality, and the experience of the enquiry process itself. Venues that respond within hours, not days, have a significant edge.

Venue Types

Conference centres dominate — but the mix is shifting

60% of won bookings in 2025 went to conference centres, up from 51% in 2022. But bars, restaurants, and hotels are all steadily growing their share.

Emerging trends

Bars: Growing from 8.7% → 11.6% of bookings. The social, informal event is on the rise — corporates want experiences, not boardrooms.
Hotels: Jumped from 17.9% to 21.2% in 2025 after being flat. The all-inclusive package is increasingly appealing for multi-day events.
Meeting Rooms: Declining from 22.2% → 18.4%. As hybrid work normalises, companies are booking fewer standalone meeting rooms and opting for more distinctive spaces.
Venue Pricing

What different venue types command

Historic and landmark venues achieve the highest booking values, while meeting rooms sit at the lower end. Your venue type doesn't define your ceiling — but it sets the starting point.

For venues: Conference centres handle the most volume (1,447 bookings) at a solid £6,825 median. But historic venues achieve £10,852 — nearly 60% more — with less volume. Premium positioning works, but you need to win the enquiry first.
Takeaways

What this means for you

🎯 For Event Organisers

Start your venue search 3–5 months ahead for conferences, 3 months for parties. The median booking value is £7,230 — budget accordingly. Consider that 55% of organisers are comparing 2+ venues, so make your brief clear and specific to get better-matched proposals.

🏛️ For Venues

Every enquiry is more competitive — you're being compared against 6+ other venues. Respond fast, personalise your proposals, and differentiate on experience. Thursday is your biggest day, September–December is your peak season. Staff and price accordingly.

📈 The Spending Story

Per-person spend has risen 62% in four years to £102. This isn't inflation alone — it reflects a shift toward higher-quality, experience-led events. Companies are booking fewer but better events, and they're willing to pay for them.

🔄 The Venue Mix

Conference centres are consolidating their dominance (60%), but the real movement is in bars and restaurants growing steadily. Meeting rooms are losing share as companies seek more inspiring spaces. The informal, social event is the growth story.
